With this sound, I was aiming to recreate a trilling bird call. Songbirds can create complex songs because they have a syrinx, which is like the human larynx but with two air passages instead of one. This allows birds to sing two notes at once and be very agile in their songs. To recreate this, I used to separate sources and put them through separate resonant highpass filters. I chose resonant filters to have the same effect as the vibrating membranes of the syrinx. For both tones, I used FM synthesis to produce two overlapping trills. The final result is a quick, two-toned trill.
